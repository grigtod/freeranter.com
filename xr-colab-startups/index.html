XR Collaboration Startups or: How I Learned to Stop Working and Love the Competition

XR‌ collaboration may be the future of work. People and companies can save a lot of time and money from commuting, renting an office and long-distance business travel. I wanted to work on this and created a simple collaboration prototype that runs locally on multiple Oculus Quests and a PC. Unfortunately, this was a mistake. I should have done more research on the competition. In this post, I’ll look into all the existing solutions and reflect on my mistake.

There are many XR collaboration solutions available, but businesses are slow to implement in their daily dealings. Either these solutions aren’t very useful or perhaps they’re prohibitively expensive to integrate. Maybe most people don’t even know about them. Do you know what such collaborative VR‌, MR and AR solutions can offer?‌

It was curious to see what’s the state-of-the-art as of Winter 2019 and what’s missing from the market. It’s important to avoid other people’s mistakes and not repeat work that’s already done, and therefore I‌ made a list of all promising solutions.

AR solutions:

Spatial | NY, USA | $8.3M funding |‌ Not yet released | Video link

A well-funded startup that focuses on augmented reality and can offer a very nice and polished UX, good integrations with external tools and innovative avatar creation pipeline. It targets collaboration in R&D, design, and brainstorming.

Prism | Oregon, USA | Not released yet | Video link

Great for architecture and planning. Cross-platform capabilities and nice UX.

ARVizio | Ottawa, Canada | Request a quote | Video link

Another AR‌ solution that aims to disrupt many industries such as Architecture, Engineering and Construction.

 

VR solutions:

Sketchbox | SF, USA | $120K funding |‌ Free | Video link

Focused on rapid prototyping, great UX and funded by Y Combinator.

VISIONxR | Brighton, UK‌ |‌ £400K funding |‌ Request a demo | Video link

Great UX and cross-platform features. Suitable for training. ‌

VIZIBLE | California, USA |‌ Made by WorldViz |‌ Request a quote | Video link

A powerful tool intended for sales, marketing and education. You can create interactions, slides and animations.

Rumii | Washington, USA‌|‌ Free | Video link

Great for presentations and education. If you’d like to teach a class remotely this is probably your best bet. Available on a wide variety of devices.

Atom | Hyderabad, India | $500K funding |‌ Not yet released | Video link

It targets multiple use cases such as training, customer service and design reviews.

Trezi | Noida, India | $2M funding |‌ $200pm or $1,920pa | Video link

Tool for architecture with the emphasis on quick design changes. Capable of generating 360 panoramas for easy sharing.

TheWild | Portland, USA |‌ Request a quote | Video link

Great for prototyping and ideation. It features a novel world scaling system designed for collaboration.

Virtualist | Szczecin, Poland |‌ Beta available, €44pm or €440pa | Video link

Good cross-platform support, simple to use and clean interface.

MeetinVR | Copenhagen, Denmark |‌ Not yet released | Video link

Great way to organize business meetings, but also good when applied to education and prototyping. It can also be used for social hangout hub with friends and family.

irisVR | NY‌, USA |‌ $13.7M funding | Billed either monthly ($75, $225, $340) or annually ($600, $1800, $2700, $4200) | Video link

This solution aims to capture the architecture market. It provides a wide variety of tools to give feedback and view architectural models in VR. It’s the most well-funded product on this list.

Improov³ | Paris, France |‌ Yearly subscription to create meetings (€4800, €9600 or $5300, $10600)

A‌ collaboration solution that targets engineering use cases.

Holodeck | California, USA |‌ Made by Nvidia |‌ Not released yet | Video link

Large feature-set and amazing graphics.

Dream | SF, USA‌‌ |‌ Free | Video link

Very simple and lacks some important features, but it’s free. It only supports 2D media.

glue | Helsinki, Finland |‌ Convoluted yearly pricing | Video link

Another solution for conferencing and prototyping.

Sync |Taiwan |‌Vive |‌ Not released yet |‌ Video not available

Not much information available.

Spaceform | London &‌ NY‌ |‌ Undisclosed | Video link

Yet to demonstrate a working application.

   

As you can see, there are many well funded and feature-rich solutions for collaboration in VR, MR and AR. Most systems target engineering, education, business meetings, prototyping and design. I think that creating an XR‌ collaboration tool that appeals to the masses is a waste of time because of the fierce competition, but there must be many niches opportunities to create software solutions that only target a certain audience and solve their problems. I will try to find such niche and provide them with a solution tailored to their needs.
